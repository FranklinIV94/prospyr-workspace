## Core Scientific Hypotheses

### A. Metabolic Similarity (Anaerobiosis)

Both parasitic worms in intestines and malignant tumors exhibit **pronounced anaerobic metabolism**:

- Both deposit large quantities of **glycogen**
- Both classified as **"amphibiotic euryoxybiotic-aerofermenter type"**
- Can survive in low-oxygen environments
- Use "incomplete oxidations" even when oxygen is present

### B. Cross-Reactivity of Synthetic Compounds

| Compound | Year | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Myracyl D** (alkylated aminoxanthone) | 1938 (H. Mauss) | Effective against Bilharzia (parasitic flatworm) AND malignant tumors |
| **Guanozolo** (5-amino-7-hydroxy-1-v-triazolo/d/pyrimidine) | G. Kidder | Suppresses nucleic acid synthesis in infusoria (same mechanism as mouse tumors) |

**Optical Activity Note:** Guanozolo is "dextrarotatory" (right-handed) — believed to be key to effectiveness.

### C. The "Atebrin" Experiments

**Researchers:** Professor V. V. Alpatov and O. K. Nastyukova

**Drug:** Atebrin (anti-malarial)

**Finding:**
| Tissue Type | More Sensitive To |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Most healthy animals | Levorotatory (left-handed) Atebrin |
| **Ehrlich's adenocarcinoma** (mouse cancer) | Dextrorotatory (right-handed) Atebrin |
| **Nematodes** (parasitic worms) | Dextrorotatory (right-handed) Atebrin |
| Certain mollusks (left-handed shells) | Dextrorotatory (right-handed) Atebrin |

**Conclusion:** "Opposite reaction" to optical molecules distinguishes malignant tumors and parasites from healthy tissue.

## The "Parasitic Nature of Tumors" Theory

### The Logic Chain

1. **Behavior:** Tumors and worms both grow in low-oxygen environments, store glycogen
2. **Chemistry:** Both uniquely susceptible to "right-handed" (dextrorotatory) molecules; healthy tissue prefers "left-handed"
3. **Treatment:** Anti-parasitic drugs (Myracyl D, Atebrin) should theoretically treat cancer

### Key Characteristics Shared

| Property | Tumors | Parasites |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Metabolism | Anaerobic | Anaerobic |
| Glycogen storage | High | High |
| Oxygen requirement | Low | Low |
| Optical molecule sensitivity | Dextrorotatory | Dextrorotatory |

## Historical Context

### CIA Interest (Two-Fold)
1. **Medical Discovery:** Had Soviets found a "cure" for cancer overlooked by Western medicine?
2. **Strategic Monitoring:** Soviet biochemical advancements → implications for biological warfare or pharmaceutical independence

### Scientific Perspective
- This report captures Soviet oncology view of cancer as a biological "invader" (not genetic mutation)
- Represents pre-molecular biology era thinking
- Later superseded by viral, genetic, and environmental cancer theories
