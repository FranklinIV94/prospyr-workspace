# Functional recovery of the adult murine hippocampus after cryopreservation by vitrification

> **Significance:** While the brain is considered exceptionally sensitive, this study shows that the hippocampus can resume electrophysiological activity after being rendered completely immobile in a cryogenic glass. The work extends known biophysical tolerance limits for brain tissue from the hypothermic to the cryogenic range.

## Key Findings

### Primary Achievement
- **First demonstration** of functional recovery of adult mammalian brain tissue after cryopreservation by vitrification
- Recovery of:
  - Structural integrity
  - Metabolic responsiveness  
  - Neuronal excitability
  - Synaptic transmission and plasticity
  - **Long-term potentiation (LTP)** was well preserved

### Protocol Details

**Vitrification Solution (V3):**
- 22.3% w/v dimethyl sulfoxide
- 16.84% w/v ethylene glycol
- 12.86% w/v formamide
- 7% w/v polyvinylpyrrolidone K12

**Temperature Protocol:**
- Cooling rate: ~130°C/s
- Warming rate: ~80°C/s
- Storage: Liquid nitrogen or -150°C freezer
- Duration: 10 minutes to 7 days

### Electrophysiology Results

| Metric | Control | Post-Vitrification | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------------------|-------|
| CA1 LTP | 157.68 ± 7.14% | 138.06 ± 6.90% | Not significantly different |
| fPSP I-O | Baseline | Reduced at max response | Trend toward lower gain |
| STP | Normal | Attenuated | Short-term plasticity reduced |
| mPP-GC LTP | 125.04 ± 5.03% | 147.96 ± 7.20% | Enhanced in postvitrification |

### Cellular Properties

**CA1 Pyramidal Cells:**
- RMP: -74.82 ± 1.12 mV (virtually identical to control)
- Increased rheobase (reduced excitability)
- Reduced AP firing at lower currents

**DG Granule Cells:**
- Slight depolarization of RMP
- Maintained discharge properties
- Enhanced excitability in some conditions

## Relevance to Research

### Applications
1. **Distribution of neuroscientific experiments** across time/locations
2. **Improved reproducibility** and animal welfare
3. **Cryosubstitution** for structural analysis in near-native state
4. **Histological examinations** and connectomics

### Breakthrough Significance
- Extends brain tolerance limits from hypothermic to cryogenic range
- Demonstrates brain can recover after complete cessation of molecular mobility
- Supports the tenet that memory is structurally encoded in synaptic architecture

## Technical Notes

### Challenges Addressed
1. **CPA toxicity** - minimized through optimized loading protocol
2. **Osmotic stress** - managed with mannitol in unloading
3. **Crystallization** - avoided through adequate CPA concentration
4. **Physical cracking** - prevented via directional cooling

### Limitations
- Short observation period (10-15 hours) for acute brain slices
- Low success rate for whole-brain vitrification
- Not directly translatable to larger organs

## Detailed Methods Summary

### Animals
- **Species:** Adult C57BL/6 J mice (3-9 months)
- **Housing:** Standard conditions

### Brain Slice Preparation
1. Horizontal 350-µm brain slices containing hippocampus
2. Prepared in ice-cold sucrose-aCSF
3. Recovered in aCSF before experimentation

### Vitrification Protocol (Slice)

**CPA Loading Steps:**
1. LM5 carrier solution (22.6 mM potassium) at 10°C
2. Progressive V3 concentrations: 0% → 2% → 4% → 8% → 16% → 30% → 45% → 59% (3 iterations)
3. All steps at -10°C for final concentration

**Cooling:**
- Directional cooling on liquid-nitrogen-cooled copper cylinder
- Estimated cooling rate: ~130°C/s
- Mesh insert method to prevent physical cracking

**Storage:**
- Liquid nitrogen (-196°C) or -150°C freezer
- Duration: 10 minutes to 7 days

**Rewarming:**
- Rapid rewarming in 52% V3 at -10°C
- Estimated warming rate: ~80°C/s

**CPA Unloading:**
- LM5 with additional 300 mM mannitol (reduces osmotic swelling)
- Progressive dilution: 45% → 30% → 16% → 8% → 4% → 2% → 0% V3
- Final LM5 without mannitol

### Whole Brain Vitrification (In Situ)
1. **Craniectomy** performed
2. **Transaortic perfusion** with:
   - Phosphate-buffered saline (PBS)
   - LM5 carrier solution
   - V3 vitrification solution
3. **Interleaved equilibration** - alternating between full-strength V3 and LM5
4. **Immersion cooling** to -140°C (1-8 days)
5. **Rapid rewarming**
6. **Hyperoncotic washout** with dextran

### Electrophysiology
- **Extracellular field recordings:** fPSP in CA1 stratum radiatum
- **Whole-cell patch-clamp:** Current-clamp and voltage-clamp
- **Stimulation:** Schaffer collaterals (SC-CA1) and medial perforant path (mPP-DG)

### Metabolic Analysis
- Seahorse XFe96 analyzer
- Oxygen consumption rate (OCR) measurements
- CA1 region biopsies

### Morphology
- **Nissl staining:** 30 µm hippocampal sections
- **Electron microscopy:** 50 nm ultrathin sections
- **Confocal imaging:** YFP-labeled pyramidal cells

## Key Technical Innovations

| Innovation | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| Mesh insert method | Prevents physical cracking during cooling |
| Directional cooling | Reduces thermomechanical stress |
| Mannitol in unloading | Reduces osmotic swelling |
| Interleaved equilibration (whole brain) | Manages CPA/water permeability mismatch |
| Hyperoncotic washout | Prevents cerebral edema |
