### The Truth About Peptides - Counter to Martin Shkreli (Mar 18)
- **Thesis:** Peptides are underexplored despite pharmaceutical industry dismissal
- **Where Shkreli is right:** Shallow understanding in community, purity issues, weak pre-clinical data
- **Counter-arguments:**
  1. **Safety:** Endogenous molecules (7,000+ in body) have different risk profiles than xenobiotic drugs
  2. **Target specificity:** Many peptides have known targets (GLP-1, Thymosin Alpha-1, PT-141)
  3. **Evolutionary optimization:** Natural selection optimized over millions of years
- **Half-life argument:** overstated - Semaglutide has 7-day half-life; short half-life can be a feature, not bug
- **Why pharma hasn't researched peptides:** Patent problem, injection barrier, wellness focus shift
- **Key quote:** "The next chapter of human health doesn't come from close-minded consensus-seeking"
- **Takeaway:** Peptide movement is messy but directionally correct about body's molecular toolkit

- **x402**: HTTP 402 protocol implementation — server returns 402, agent pays on-chain (USDC on Base/Tempo), retries with payment proof, server delivers resource
