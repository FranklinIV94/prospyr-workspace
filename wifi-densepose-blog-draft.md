# WiFi Sensing: The Invisible Technology That Can See Through Walls

*What business owners need to know about the latest advancement in wireless privacy*

---

## Introduction

Imagine a technology that can detect human presence, track movements, and even monitor vital signs — without a single camera. No wearables. No sensors. Just existing WiFi signals in your building.

This isn't science fiction. It's WiFi DensePose, and it's available today.

## How It Works

Every time you move, you disturb the WiFi signals around you. These disturbances — called Channel State Information (CSI) — contain incredibly detailed data about what's happening in a space.

By analyzing CSI data, advanced systems can reconstruct:
- **Human pose** — body position and movement in real-time
- **Vital signs** — breathing rate and heartbeat
- **Presence detection** — even through walls

The math is complex, but the takeaway is simple: your existing WiFi network is quietly broadcasting information about everyone inside your building.

## Real-World Applications

This technology has legitimate uses:
- Elderly monitoring and fall detection
- Smart building automation  
- Security and access control
- Infant monitoring

But the same technology raises serious privacy concerns.

## The Privacy Risk

Here's what most business owners don't realize: **standard security cameras can be avoided. WiFi signals cannot.**

Anyone with the right equipment (ESP32-S3 modules cost ~$54) can:
- Detect how many people are in a room
- Track movement between rooms
- Monitor breathing patterns (vital signs)
- Operate through standard walls

This is particularly concerning for:
- Executive offices
- Conference rooms
- Healthcare facilities
- Any space where sensitive conversations occur

## How to Protect Yourself

1. **Understand your environment** — WiFi-based sensing requires CSI-capable hardware, which is increasingly accessible

2. **Consider signal containment** — professional RF shielding for critical spaces

3. **Segment your network** — isolate sensitive areas on separate networks

4. **Hardware inspection** — regularly check for unauthorized wireless devices

5. **Policy awareness** — educate leadership about emerging surveillance technologies
