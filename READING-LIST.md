  - "Oil of the Pharaohs" = Nigella sativa, used ~3,000 years (Ebers Papyrus 1550 BCE)
  - Claims: Increases glutathione, improves insulin sensitivity, clears amyloid, reduces inflammation, anticancer potential, etc.
  - Primary active compound: **Thymoquinone (TQ)** — neutralizes free radicals, activates Nrf2 pathway, suppresses NF-κB, anti-cancer
  - Other compounds: Thymol (membrane disruption, antimicrobial), Carvacrol (biofilm inhibition), p-Cymene, Nigellone

  - Contraindications: Pregnant (uterine contractions), low BP, blood thinners, gut damage
  - Historical: Cleopatra used it; Muhammad: "heals every disease except death"; Ibn Sina praised it
  - Tone: Science-heavy, detailed biochemistry, cited mechanisms
